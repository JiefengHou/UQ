Copyright Disclaimer

  • Purpose

    To ensure compliance with the Copyright Laws and not expose the Company and its employees to civil or criminal liability from copyright infringement; to ensure compliance with the contractual provisions relating to copyrights; and to protect Company property by securing copyrights.

  • Definitions

    • Copyright

      The exclusive rights to reproduce, prepare derivative works, publish, perform, and distribute the matter and form of a literary, artistic, or other copyrightable work. Computer programs and documentation are copyrightable works. Notice of whether a document is copyrighted and the identity of the copyright owner are generally found on the title page or the page immediately following, or near the beginning or end of a work.

    • Copyright Infringement

      Merely rephrasing or re-organizing copyrighted material will not avoid copyright infringement; neither will limiting distribution to within the Company or the Government, nor giving credit to the copyright owner.

  • Policy

    • Use of Copyrighted Material

      • Authors and Technical Writers should not incorporate material copyrighted by others in reports, brochures, manuals, or other publications that they prepare. Government contracts generally prohibit including any copyrighted material in data to be delivered unless a license for Government use is obtained.

      • Under the current law, one copy of a copyrighted publication for private study or research may be made without obtaining the permission of the copyright holder.

    • Protection of Company Originated Materials

      When a report, book, chart, computer program, and other documentation is prepared within the Company, the desirability of obtaining copyright protection should be reviewed by the President, and/or a Company Official appointed by the President, prior to publication, if not obvious in view of the following:

      • ACopyright protection may be desirable to identify the Company as the source, or to aid in commercial sale or licensing of the copyrighted work.

      • Copyright protection may not be desirable when it is expected that free licenses will be given to others; e.g., the Government.

  • Procedure

    • Employees desiring permission to incorporate copyrighted material in a document should provide the President, and/or a Company Official appointed by the President, with identification of the specific material desired to be copied, and the use for which permission is sought. Permission is more likely to be given freely by copyright owners where distribution will be internal, or without charge. Where the document to be produced is intended to be sold or used commercially, a free license might not be given.

    • If a copy of copyrighted material is made, an extra copy of the first page of the material should also be made, which identifies the journal or book, the publication date, and the copy fee if any is printing on the publication. Your name and the number of copies made, as well as the number of pages in the article, should be handwritten on the extra copy. The extra copy should be delivered to the President, and/or a Company Official appointed by the President, who will then process royalty payment to the publisher if not covered by Corporate license with the Copyright Clearance Center.
